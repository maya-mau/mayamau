What I Do

  1. BreakthroughTech AI

    As a BreakthroughTech AI fellow in summer 2023 and into this academic school year, I was accepted into a cohort of fellow women and non-binary students and was given the opportunity to learn and apply machine learning techniques through multiple projects. After enrolling in an intensive, online 10-week Machine Learning Foundations course from Cornell Tech to learn data preprocessing steps, machine learning models, and gain analytical skills, I worked with the MIT & IBM Watson Lab to build a chat assistant using large language models with the goal to create accessible, less expensive chatbots. Now, I am competing alongside a team of my peers in a Kaggle competition sponsored by the New York City Botanical Gardens to build a model that can sort images based on plant species.

  2. The Radhakrishnan Lab

    Last summer, I got the opportunity to work at the Radhakrishnan Lab at Wellesley College. My project involved designing and modeling antimicrobial peptides to increase their efficacy, due to the increasing issue of antibiotic resistance. In addition to strengthening my chemistry knowledge, I was able to apply my Python programming skills to automate complex processes in the shell.
